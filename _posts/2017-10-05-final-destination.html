    Lost and confused I touched down at Sydney airport. Most of the confusion stemmed from the fact that I slept during the last 20 minutes of the flight, resulting in the cabin crew deciding to not hand me the form about what possessions I would like to declare when entering the country.

    Seeing all the passenger walking around the yellow forms in their hands made my anxiety levels heighten a bit, but a quick inquiry with the staff working the customs made it clear it was no biggie, it would be available further ahead.

    I made my way down to the luggage extraction conveyor belts, where I waited around for my luggage to arrive. The luggage started arriving, but my backpack was nowhere in sight. After having waited around for about 15 minutes I started feeling worried again, but five minutes later it finally arrived.

  • Customs

    Making my way down to the customs I prepared myself for a long process of declaring my possessions before I could enter the country, after all I had watched some episodes of border security Australia before going here...

    I started filling out the yellow form, carefully making sure I had understood everything on it correct, then I handed it to one of the customs staff, who took a quick glance at it, then pointed me in a new direction to go. Perfect all I would need to do now is to pass the individual questioning of new arrivals, then I would be able to enter the country, at least that's how it works on TV.

    To my confusion I found myself in the "arrivals" section of the airport. No questioning or anything.

    I was in.

  • Getting to the Hostel

    I connected my phone to the airport-wifi and started looking for a way to get to the hostel, apparently it would be located in the centre of Sydney, it's small thing like that, that I've ought to have looked up beforehand.

    Luckily for me the trains ran just under the airport which made getting to Sydney central a rather trivial matter. A one way ticket later, and I was on my way.

    The trains were larger than ones I had seen i Sweden, they also had two levels and a cool system for adjusting the back of the seat. In hindsight I wish I had remembered to take a picture of it, but by this point 20 - 30 hours of sleep deprivation had made my brain have other priorities.

    After riding the train for about 15-20 minutes I got of at the central station. I was then I came to the realization that I had no idea of where to go, it wouldn't be as easy as to look it up on google maps neither, as I hadn't gotten any phone plan yet.

  • About 20 uneventfull minutes of navigating my way around the block like it was the 90's, I finally found the hostel. I walked up to the reception and was immediately uppgraded to a 4-share dorm instead of the 8-share one I had booked. Score! :D

    Deciding to first drop off my luggage, I then proceded to the rooftop where the was rumored to be a bar. I needed a drink!

    I stepped out on the ninth floor and into a ...mexican poolparty?? :O Everywhere there were people dressed in sombreros drinking drinks listening to EDM music, at one point some people even decided to jump into the pool with their clothes on.

    I couldn't handle this at the moment, so I stepped out on the "not-so-party"-balcony and decided to call my parents to let them know I was still alive. Meanwhile i took a photo of the view.

    Maybe tomorrow I would join the mexican-madness...
